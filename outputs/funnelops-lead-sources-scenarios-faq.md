# FunnelOps Lead Sources, Scenarios, and FAQ

## Lead Source Map

FunnelOps should start with lead sources that solo sellers already use every day. The early product does not need every real integration on day one; the demo should show the workflow across realistic channels, then make integrations the SaaS expansion path.

### Tier 1: Core Demo Sources

These should be in the hackathon demo first.

1. **Social DMs**
   - Instagram DM
   - LinkedIn DM
   - Facebook Messenger
   - TikTok DM
   - X/Twitter DM
   - Why it matters: high-intent leads often arrive informally and get lost.

2. **Social Comments**
   - Instagram post comment
   - LinkedIn comment
   - TikTok comment
   - YouTube comment
   - Facebook group/comment
   - Why it matters: comments are public buying signals that need fast conversion into private follow-up.

3. **Web Forms / Landing Pages**
   - Contact form
   - Consultation request
   - Quote request
   - Lead magnet signup
   - Event/webinar inquiry
   - Why it matters: this is easiest to integrate and easiest for judges to understand.

4. **Email Inquiries**
   - Gmail inbox
   - Website contact email
   - Referral intro email
   - Newsletter reply
   - Why it matters: email is still a common place where serious buyers ask detailed questions.

5. **WhatsApp-Style Chat**
   - WhatsApp
   - Telegram
   - SMS/iMessage-style inquiry
   - Website chat transcript
   - Why it matters: many small businesses close sales through chat, especially in Malaysia/SEA.

### Tier 2: SaaS Expansion Sources

These are strong after the hackathon.

6. **Booking Tools**
   - Calendly
   - Google Calendar appointments
   - TidyCal
   - Acuity
   - Why it matters: connects inquiry to booked call and post-call follow-up.

7. **CRM / Pipeline Imports**
   - HubSpot
   - Airtable
   - Notion
   - Google Sheets
   - Pipedrive
   - Why it matters: lets FunnelOps become the memory and action layer without replacing every tool.

8. **Ad Lead Forms**
   - Meta Lead Ads
   - LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms
   - Google Ads lead forms
   - TikTok lead forms
   - Why it matters: paid leads are expensive, so missed follow-up has clear ROI.

9. **Community / Group Leads**
   - Facebook Groups
   - Discord
   - Slack community
   - Circle/Skool community
   - Why it matters: many creators and service sellers generate demand through communities.

10. **Call / Meeting Notes**
   - Sales call notes
   - Voice memo summary
   - Zoom/Meet transcript
   - Consultation notes
   - Why it matters: memory becomes much stronger when it learns from conversations and outcomes.

## Demo Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Instagram DM From a Local Service Business

**Source:** Instagram DM  
**Avatar:** Local Service Operator  
**Example lead:** "Hi, I saw your post about launch strategy. I run a small skincare clinic and need help turning IG inquiries into bookings. Do you do this and what would it cost?"

**Agent should identify:**
- Intent: service inquiry + pricing request.
- Urgency: medium.
- Buyer context: local service business.
- Missing info: current inquiry volume, offer, booking process, timeline.
- Memory needed: pricing guardrail, human approval rule, tone preference, best-fit ICP.

**Best action:**
Draft a warm reply offering a free discovery call before giving a quote.

**Human approval needed because:**
Lead asked about cost/pricing.

### Scenario 2: Boutique Agency Overwhelmed by Follow-Ups

**Source:** Website form  
**Avatar:** Boutique Agency Owner  
**Example lead:** "We are a 4-person design agency. Leads come from referrals but we keep forgetting follow-ups. Can your AI help qualify and draft replies before we send them?"

**Agent should identify:**
- Intent: product fit inquiry.
- Pain: missed follow-ups and founder overload.
- Budget signal: not stated.
- Missing info: monthly lead volume, channels, approval process.
- Memory needed: best-fit ICP, tone preference, approval rule.

**Best action:**
Draft a reply confirming fit and ask two qualification questions.

### Scenario 3: LinkedIn Comment From Solo B2B Founder

**Source:** LinkedIn comment  
**Avatar:** Solo B2B Founder  
**Example lead:** "This is exactly my problem. I sell B2B consulting and have calls scattered across LinkedIn, email, and WhatsApp. Need something lightweight, not a huge CRM."

**Agent should identify:**
- Intent: strong pain/problem match.
- Pain: scattered channels, heavy CRM avoidance.
- Urgency: medium-high.
- Missing info: sales process, tools used, volume.
- Memory needed: ICP wedge and positioning.

**Best action:**
Draft a private follow-up message positioning FunnelOps as a lightweight inbound command center.

### Scenario 4: Email Referral Asking for a Quote

**Source:** Referral email  
**Avatar:** Creator-Consultant or Boutique Agency Owner  
**Example lead:** "Caroline, my friend said you can help set up an AI lead follow-up system. Can you send pricing and what is included?"

**Agent should identify:**
- Intent: referral quote request.
- Budget signal: asks pricing directly.
- Risk: quote should not be sent automatically.
- Missing info: business type, number of lead channels, urgency, desired outcome.

**Best action:**
Draft a short reply thanking them for the referral and asking discovery questions before quote.

### Scenario 5: Webinar / Lead Magnet Signup Reply

**Source:** Lead magnet reply or webinar signup  
**Avatar:** Creator-Consultant  
**Example lead:** "I downloaded your guide. I need help making my DMs less chaotic. Do you have a service for this?"

**Agent should identify:**
- Intent: nurtured lead, warmer than cold inquiry.
- Pain: DM chaos.
- Missing info: channels, offer type, volume.
- Memory needed: retired webinar promo should be downgraded or ignored if stale.

**Best action:**
Draft a helpful reply and route to discovery call or checklist.

## Customer FAQ

### What is FunnelOps Autopilot?

FunnelOps Autopilot is an AI inbound sales assistant for solo sellers and small teams. It turns messy leads from DMs, comments, forms, email, and chat into structured next actions: qualification, draft replies, follow-up plans, and human-approved sales workflows.

### Who is it for?

It is for solo service sellers, creator-consultants, boutique agency owners, local service operators, and founder-led B2B sellers who get real inbound interest but do not have a dedicated sales team.

### Who is it not for?

It is not meant to replace Salesforce for large enterprise teams. It is also not for spammy outbound automation or businesses with no inbound leads yet.

### Does the AI send messages automatically?

Not by default. Important actions such as quotes, discounts, commitments, high-value leads, or uncertain replies go to a human approval queue first.

### Why is memory important?

Most AI tools treat every message like a new conversation. FunnelOps remembers business rules, owner preferences, lead context, pricing guardrails, past objections, and what worked before, so future responses can improve.

### What does the human approval queue do?

It gives the owner final control before important messages go out. The agent can draft and recommend, but the owner can approve, edit, or reject.

### Can it connect to Instagram, WhatsApp, Gmail, or forms?

The hackathon prototype starts with realistic mocked sources and backend workflows. The SaaS version can expand into real integrations such as Instagram, WhatsApp, Gmail, website forms, calendars, CRMs, and Google Sheets.

### Is this a CRM?

Not exactly. It is closer to a lightweight inbound sales command center. It can work with a CRM, but its main job is to understand inbound leads, retrieve memory, recommend next actions, and help the owner respond faster.

### How is it different from an AI SDR?

Most AI SDR tools focus on outbound prospecting or enterprise sales teams. FunnelOps focuses on inbound leads for solo sellers and small operators who already have demand but need better follow-up, memory, and control.

### What does the agent track?

It tracks lead source, intent, urgency, budget signal, objections, missing information, selected memories, draft replies, approval decisions, follow-up plans, token usage, and workflow history.

### Can I see why the agent made a decision?

Yes. The agent trace shows the workflow stages: capture, qualify, retrieve memory, draft action, approval gate, and learning loop.

### What happens when a memory is outdated?

Outdated or low-confidence memories can be downgraded or excluded from context. This prevents the agent from using old promotions, stale pricing, or retired offers.

### How does this save money or time?

It reduces missed leads, repeated manual qualification, slow replies, inconsistent follow-ups, and founder overload. It also shows token usage so AI cost can be monitored.

### Is my API key safe?

The Qwen Cloud API key stays server-side. The browser never receives the key, and the app calls its own backend endpoint rather than Qwen Cloud directly.

## Judge FAQ

### Which hackathon track is this for?

Primary track: Track 4 - Autopilot Agent. It automates an end-to-end business workflow from inbound inquiry to qualification, draft action, human approval, trace, and learning loop.

### Where does Qwen Cloud fit?

Qwen Cloud powers reasoning, classification, memory-aware drafting, next-action planning, and structured sales recommendations through the backend.

### Where does memory fit?

The system stores business memories and selects relevant memories using importance, confidence, freshness, and text relevance. It also marks stale memories so they are not blindly retrieved.

### Where is the human-in-the-loop checkpoint?

The approval queue requires the owner to approve or reject important sales actions before they are treated as completed.

### What is the production-readiness angle?

The app separates frontend from backend, keeps the Qwen API key server-side, logs workflow runs, shows token usage, includes a health endpoint, and has a deployment proof plan for Alibaba Cloud.

### How do judges know it is not just a one-shot prompt?

The dashboard shows retrieved memories, trace steps, token usage, workflow persistence, approval decisions, and stale-memory handling.

### What has been verified so far?

The local prototype has passed automated tests, production build, browser QA, live Qwen Cloud UI verification, and persistence/memory operations checks.
