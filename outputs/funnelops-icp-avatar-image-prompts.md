# FunnelOps ICP + Customer Journey Image Prompts

Use these prompts in Gemini, ChatGPT image generation, or another visual tool.

## Prompt 1: ICP + Customer Avatar Board

Create a polished 16:9 strategic customer avatar board for a SaaS product called **FunnelOps Autopilot**.

Product context:
FunnelOps Autopilot is an AI-powered inbound sales autopilot for solo creators, consultants, coaches, boutique agencies, and small service businesses. It turns messy inbound leads from DMs, comments, forms, email, and WhatsApp-style chats into structured sales actions: qualification, memory-aware replies, quotes, follow-ups, booking suggestions, and human approval checkpoints.

Visual goal:
Show the Ideal Customer Profile and 4 customer avatars in a clear, business-strategy style. This should feel like a founder strategy board, not a cute persona poster. Make it useful for hackathon judges, product planning, and SaaS positioning.

Title:
**FunnelOps Autopilot: ICP + Customer Avatars**

Subtitle:
The best early users are solo sellers with real inbound demand, scattered conversations, and no sales team.

Central ICP box:
**Primary ICP: Solo service seller / creator-consultant**
- Gets leads from social content, DMs, referrals, forms, and chat.
- Sells services, packages, consults, audits, quotes, or appointments.
- Has no dedicated sales team.
- Loses revenue through slow replies, inconsistent follow-up, and scattered lead context.
- Wants AI assistance but still wants human approval before important messages go out.

Create 4 avatar cards:

1. **The Creator-Consultant**
Profile:
- Sells coaching, consulting, courses, audits, or advisory calls.
- Gets leads from LinkedIn, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, newsletters, and webinars.
Pain:
- Good leads get buried in DMs and comments.
- Repeats the same qualification questions manually.
- Forgets context from past conversations.
Buying trigger:
- Missed a high-value lead or had a viral post create too many inquiries.
Success metric:
- Faster lead response, more booked calls, better follow-up consistency.

2. **The Boutique Agency Owner**
Profile:
- Runs a small marketing, design, automation, web, or AI services agency.
- Handles inbound leads while also managing delivery.
Pain:
- Too many custom inquiries.
- Hard to qualify budget, scope, urgency, and fit.
- Proposals and follow-ups fall through the cracks.
Buying trigger:
- Pipeline is active but messy; founder is tired of being the manual sales coordinator.
Success metric:
- Cleaner qualification, faster quote drafts, fewer missed follow-ups.

3. **The Local Service Operator**
Profile:
- Runs a local service business such as clinic, beauty studio, repair, events, education, or home services.
- Gets leads from ads, Google, WhatsApp, Facebook, forms, and referrals.
Pain:
- Customers ask repetitive questions.
- Staff responses vary.
- Booking and quote follow-up are inconsistent.
Buying trigger:
- Paid ads generate leads but conversion is weak.
Success metric:
- More bookings, better response time, clear customer handoff.

4. **The Solo B2B Founder**
Profile:
- Early-stage founder selling B2B services, productized consulting, or a niche SaaS offer.
- Uses founder-led sales.
Pain:
- Needs CRM discipline but hates heavy CRM tools.
- Wants structured memory of buyer objections, preferences, and next steps.
- Needs a lightweight sales operating system.
Buying trigger:
- Founder needs to look more professional and stop losing context between calls.
Success metric:
- More organized pipeline, better investor/customer demo story, less founder overload.

Add comparison strip:
**Poor fit users**
- Large enterprise sales teams needing full Salesforce replacement.
- Pure outbound spam workflows.
- Businesses with no inbound leads yet.
- Users who want AI to send everything without approval.

Add wedge statement:
**Beachhead wedge: memory-rich inbound sales autopilot for people without a sales team.**

Design style:
- Dark or clean neutral background.
- 4 distinct avatar cards with simple icons.
- Clear hierarchy.
- Professional SaaS strategy board.
- Use accent colors but avoid clutter.
- Make all text readable.
- No cartoons, no fake stock photos, no excessive decorative gradients.

Output:
One 16:9 infographic image suitable for Notion, README, pitch deck, and hackathon demo planning.

## Prompt 2: Multi-Swimlane Customer Journey Map

Create a detailed 16:9 multi-swimlane customer journey map for **FunnelOps Autopilot**, a Qwen Cloud-powered AI inbound sales autopilot.

Purpose:
This image should show the end-to-end customer journey before and after FunnelOps Autopilot. It should help hackathon judges understand the real business workflow, the AI agent orchestration, the persistent memory layer, and the human-in-the-loop checkpoints.

Title:
**FunnelOps Autopilot: Customer Journey + Agent Workflow Map**

Subtitle:
How messy inbound interest becomes qualified, memory-aware sales action.

Primary scenario:
A solo creator-consultant receives several inbound leads from social posts, DMs, comments, forms, and email after launching a new offer. FunnelOps Autopilot helps classify, remember, draft, approve, and learn from each interaction.

Use a left-to-right journey with 7 stages:

1. **Awareness / Interest**
Lead sees content, ad, referral, landing page, or offer.

2. **Messy Inbound**
Lead sends a DM, comment, form, email, or chat message.

3. **Qualification**
Agent extracts intent, budget, urgency, fit, objections, and missing information.

4. **Memory Retrieval**
Agent retrieves relevant business rules, owner preferences, past lead context, pricing rules, and follow-up patterns.

5. **Sales Action Draft**
Agent proposes reply, quote, booking suggestion, follow-up plan, or CRM update.

6. **Human Approval**
Owner reviews high-impact actions, edits if needed, approves or rejects.

7. **Learning Loop**
Agent logs outcome, updates memory, improves future decisions, and tracks metrics.

Create these swim lanes:

Lane 1: **Lead / Customer**
- Sees offer
- Asks question
- Shares need or objection
- Receives helpful reply
- Books call / requests quote / buys

Lane 2: **Business Owner**
- Posts content or runs campaign
- Gets overwhelmed by scattered messages
- Reviews AI recommendation
- Approves action
- Uses dashboard to see pipeline and follow-ups

Lane 3: **FunnelOps Autopilot Agent**
- Ingests lead
- Classifies intent and urgency
- Plans next best action
- Drafts response or quote
- Schedules follow-up
- Explains reasoning in agent trace

Lane 4: **Memory Layer**
- Stores business rules
- Stores lead preferences and past context
- Scores memories by importance, recency, confidence, and source
- Retrieves only relevant memories within context budget
- Forgets or downgrades stale memories

Lane 5: **Human-in-the-Loop Control**
- Flags high-value leads
- Flags uncertainty or missing info
- Requires approval before sending important messages
- Captures owner feedback

Lane 6: **Tools / Integrations**
- Social DM or comment source
- Web form / landing page
- Email or chat inbox
- CRM / lead database
- Calendar / booking link
- Quote or proposal generator
- Alibaba Cloud backend
- Qwen Cloud model call

Lane 7: **Metrics / Outcomes**
- Response time
- Lead qualification quality
- Follow-up completion
- Booking rate
- Quote conversion
- Missed-lead reduction
- Token usage per workflow run
- Memory retrieval precision

Add a "Before FunnelOps" pain strip above or below:
- Scattered inboxes
- Slow replies
- Repeated questions
- Lost context
- Missed follow-ups
- No memory of what worked
- Founder overload

Add an "After FunnelOps" outcome strip:
- Unified lead workflow
- Faster response
- Memory-aware personalization
- Human-approved automation
- Better follow-up
- Visible agent trace
- Reusable SaaS workflow

Add a small callout:
**Hackathon proof point: Qwen Cloud powers reasoning, drafting, memory selection, and agent planning. Alibaba Cloud runs the backend.**

Design style:
- Professional technical journey map.
- Multiple horizontal swim lanes with clear row labels.
- Clean flow arrows across the 7 stages.
- Use icons sparingly.
- High readability.
- Avoid tiny text.
- Avoid cartoon style.
- Use a command-center / SaaS strategy aesthetic.

Output:
One 16:9 infographic image suitable for Notion, README, architecture explanation, and demo planning.
